Productivity Zone

The Productivity Zone is working at your highest productivity. Procrastination, fixed mindsets and perfectionism are barriers that us from being our most efficient and effective. Procrastinators tend to work first on what they want to work on, instead of what should be prioritized first, or get their important tasks done at the last minute. They may be afraid of being successful or feel they are inadequate.

You feel you procrastinate, here are some tips:

Be more organized

Record all tasks that require attention using the tool that works for you best. I like Sticky Notes on my laptop. Or use your Outlook Calendar to list when you will work on tasks in order of importance

Begin with simple goals

Break down your goals into smaller tasks that you need to do in order to achieve them. Identify how much time you have to complete the goal, then place to do items in that timeline. Try to set the deadline for a few days before it is due. This gives you time to review or tweak your work.

Do more difficult or complicated work first

Once you do the harder work that you might want to push out, guess what, it's completed! After that, the other tasks are easy.

Eliminate distractions

Is your work environment too loud? Do co-workers come to you to discuss work that is not your highest priority? Try moving to a conference room or adjusting your work hours

Time check

Test how productive you are compared to what you planned. Adjust if necessary.

Take a break

Studies have shown that adults are more productive and learn better if they take a short break every hour. This allows the brain to organize your thoughts and evaluate.

Share your goal with a mentor or co-worker

Another person can help motivate you and encourage you to meet your deadlines. They can celebrate your success, too.

Perfectionists may think that they are never good enough and may put in more work than is necessary to feel good about their efforts. They may always think they could do more or do better.

If you are a perfectionist, here are some tips:

Set realistic personal goals and expectations.

Recognize that you are already an achiever and you don't need to be perfect to continue being successful

Stop listening to negative self-talk.

Use positive feedback to achieve your goals and not a negative inner voice to hurt your emotional wellbeing and self-esteem.

Prioritize you.

Take the time to pursue what you enjoy doing. Reflect on what makes you happy and do whatever that is more often.

Saying NO

Perfectionists often struggle with saying no to new requests due to ambition or the need to please others. But having boundaries can give you the energy to get things done and work on what matters the most.

Trust yourself that the project get done on time.

You have what it takes to get tasks done. Use that knowledge to increase your confidence. Believe in yourself.

Schedule breaks and recharge.

Just like procrastinators, perfectionists often don’t take the time they need to recharge their batteries. You will feel better and work more effectively if you take breaks.

Avoid multitasking.

Multitasking drains energy, decreases efficiency, slows your memory, and increases stress. Work on one task at a time.
